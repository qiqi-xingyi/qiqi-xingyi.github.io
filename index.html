About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Kent State University and a Visiting Researcher at Cleveland Clinic. My research focuses on hardware-aware quantum workflows for protein active-site structure prediction and docking. I design frameworks that integrate real quantum processors (IBM Quantum) with classical post-processing for structural reconstruction, RMSD evaluation, and docking validation.

  • Research Interests: Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE), Quantum Sampling, Protein Active-Site Modeling
  • Keywords: quantum biology, hybrid algorithms, structure reconstruction

Research Overview

End-to-End Quantum Frameworks on Utility-Level Hardware

Building practical quantum frameworks on utility-level quantum processors. Starting from amino acid sequences, algorithms such as Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) are employed to identify low-energy conformations corresponding to stable protein structures.

Hybrid Quantum Algorithms

Integrating AI models and classical algorithms to expand the system’s information capacity, enabling quantum algorithms to surpass intrinsic QPU limitations under current hardware constraints.

Cross-Scale Integration in Drug Discovery

Designing quantum algorithms and frameworks that bridge hierarchical levels— from protein structures to molecular interactions—across different physical and chemical scales to advance quantum-enabled drug discovery.
