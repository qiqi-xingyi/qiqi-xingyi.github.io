About

I am a PhD student in Quantum Computational Biology, working at the intersection of quantum computing and the life sciences. My research bridges quantum algorithms and real-world drug discovery.

I am jointly affiliated with Kent State University (advised by Dr. Qiang Guan) and the Cleveland Clinic (co-advised by Dr. Feixiong Cheng). Previously, I was selected for the 2023 Quantum Talents Climbing Program at SZIQA, mentored by Dr. Juan Yao.

Research Focus

My work spans Variational Quantum Algorithms (VQA), Quantum-HPC hybrid frameworks, and Quantum-AI integration — applied to protein structure prediction, energy surface reconstruction, and molecular docking.

01

End-to-End Quantum Frameworks

Building practical frameworks on utility-level hardware. Utilizing algorithms like VQE on IBM Eagle (127q) and Heron (156q) processors to identify low-energy conformations for stable protein structures.

02

Hybrid Quantum-AI Algorithms

Integrating Generative AI and classical optimization to expand system information capacity. This approach allows quantum algorithms to transcend intrinsic QPU limitations and noise constraints.

03

Cross-Scale Drug Discovery

Designing algorithms that bridge hierarchical levels—from protein folding to ligand docking—across physical and chemical scales to advance the era of quantum-enabled drug design.

04

Hyper-Scale Fusion

Developing high-throughput fusion protocols that integrate massive-scale classical datasets with quantum feature spaces, leveraging HPC clusters to process "super-volume" biological data and improve the scalability of quantum simulations.
