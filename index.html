About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Kent State University. My research focuses on hardware-aware quantum workflows for protein active-site structure prediction and docking. I design frameworks that integrate real quantum processors (IBM Quantum) with classical post-processing for structural reconstruction, RMSD evaluation, and docking validation.

  • Research Interests: Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE), Quantum Sampling, Protein Active-Site Modeling
  • Keywords: quantum biology, hybrid algorithms, structure reconstruction

Research Overview

Quantum Frameworks on Real Processors

Developing end-to-end quantum computing frameworks tailored for actual hardware, capable of encoding amino-acid sequences and predicting 3D active-site structures.

Sampling-Driven Structure Reconstruction

Using quantum sampling to explore conformational states, followed by classical clustering and energy-guided refinement to recover stable protein conformations.

Evaluation and Benchmarking

Performing docking tests and RMSD analyses to validate results, and constructing benchmark datasets for assessing the practical utility of quantum algorithms.
