PhD Student in Quantum Biological Computing

Kent State University · Cleveland Clinic

I work at the intersection of quantum computing and the life sciences. My research focuses on computational methods for biological structure and drug discovery, with particular attention to what can be executed and evaluated on real quantum hardware.

I am jointly affiliated with Kent State University, advised by Dr. Qiang Guan, and Cleveland Clinic, co-advised by Dr. Feixiong Cheng. Previously, I was selected for the 2023 Quantum Talents Climbing Program at SZIQA, mentored by Dr. Juan Yao.

01Research interests

Methods and systems for computational biology on emerging quantum hardware.

Hardware-executed quantum biocomputing

Biological structure workflows designed for execution on real quantum processors, with molecular representations and physical constraints retained throughout the pipeline.

Quantum bioinformatics

Quantum-aware representations of biological sequences, structures, conformational states, and molecular interaction patterns.

Quantum–HPC systems

Integration of quantum execution, optimization, post-processing, and scientific data pipelines within high-performance computing environments.

Quantum methods for drug discovery

Applications to protein conformations, binding-site prediction, molecular docking, and ligand-response modeling.
